Best EVs Under ₹10 Lakh in India (2025)

June 15, 2025

[Lights fade in, camera pans across busy Indian city traffic. Honking, chaos, and pollution dominate the scene.]

Scene 1: Monday Morning Reality Check

It’s Monday morning, and you’re stuck once again in a chaotic jam on Delhi’s infamous ring road. Petrol fumes waft around, cars honk impatiently, and your fuel needle dangerously flirts with “Empty.”

“Not again! Didn’t I just spend ₹6,000 on fuel this week?” you mutter, frustrated.

You glance aside. A futuristic, sleek car silently glides past—effortlessly, quietly, no fumes, no drama.

“Must be expensive,” you think aloud.

Your co-passenger nudges you and whispers with a grin, “It costs less than your petrol bills! That’s an EV—fully electric and under ₹10 lakh!”

You’re intrigued. “Affordable EVs under ₹10 lakh? Really?”

[Cue dramatic music, scene transitions.]

Scene 1: Why Switch to an Electric Vehicle in India?

Before unveiling the best affordable EVs, let’s briefly revisit why millions of Indians are making this electric leap:

  • Cost Savings: With petrol reaching ₹115 per litre, charging your EV at home overnight means massive monthly savings.
  • Government Subsidies: Enjoy subsidies up to ₹1.5 lakh under India’s FAME-II scheme, significantly reducing upfront costs.
  • Clean & Green: Zero emissions, reducing India’s air pollution dramatically.

[Cut to next scene, camera pans across bustling Indian streets.]

Scene 2: India’s Best EVs Under ₹10 Lakh (2025)

Now, let’s dive into the top affordable EVs shaking up Indian roads right now:

1. Tata Tiago EV – India’s Favorite Budget EV

  • Price: ₹8.49 Lakh (ex-showroom)
  • Range: 250 km/charge
  • Battery Warranty: 8 years/160,000 km
  • Why Buy?: Proven reliability, practical range, affordable servicing.

Real-life scenario:
Rahul from Pune says, “Switching to the Tiago EV cut my monthly travel costs by almost 80%! Charging at home overnight is hassle-free and ultra-cheap.”

Pro Tip:
Opt for home charging setups for the cheapest running costs (just ₹1.5 per km).

2. MG Comet EV – The Urban Icon

  • Price: ₹7.98 Lakh
  • Range: 230 km (city ideal)
  • Standout Features: Connected technology, compact design perfect for tight city traffic.

Real-life Scenario:
Ananya from Delhi says, “The Comet EV is perfect for crowded city roads—parking is a breeze, and I never worry about fuel costs.”

Expert Insight (SIAM):
MG’s rapid expansion of urban charging infrastructure makes Comet EV a favorite among urban commuters.

3. Citroën eC3 – European Comfort Meets Indian Roads

  • Price: ₹9.99 Lakh
  • Range: Impressive 320 km
  • Highlight: Stylish exterior, spacious interiors, robust build quality.

User Review:
Rajeev from Pune shares, “We took our Citroën eC3 to Goa and back—no issues, plenty of public charging stations along highways!”

Pro Tip:
Check your state EV subsidies. In Delhi, Maharashtra, and Karnataka, additional benefits significantly lower your total cost.

4. Upcoming Tata Punch EV – The Affordable Electric SUV (Launching Late 2025)

  • Expected Price: ₹9.99 Lakh (Ex-showroom)
  • Estimated Range: 280-300 KM per charge

India’s eagerly awaiting this SUV-style EV. It promises affordability, rugged looks, and SUV practicality for everyday adventure.

Market Buzz:
Industry experts anticipate it to be a game-changer in affordable electric SUVs, much like the successful Nexon EV.

Scene 5: FAQs – Your Quick EV Questions Answered

  • Are these EVs really cheaper to maintain?
    Absolutely! Maintenance costs are about 60-70% lower than petrol vehicles due to fewer moving parts.
  • Are affordable EV batteries durable enough?
    Modern EV batteries typically last 8-10 years or 1,50,000-2,00,000 km with minimal degradation.
  • How much does home EV charging cost in India?
    Approximately ₹150-₹200 per full charge (around ₹1-₹2 per km), significantly cheaper than petrol.
