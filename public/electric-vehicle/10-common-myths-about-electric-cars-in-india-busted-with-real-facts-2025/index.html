10 Common Myths About Electric Cars in India – Busted with Real Facts (2025)

June 14, 2025

Thinking about switching to an electric vehicle (EV), but stuck with too many doubts?

You’re not alone.

Many Indians still hesitate to go electric because of myths and outdated information. But things have changed drastically in recent years. The EV industry in India is booming, and it’s time we bust these common EV myths with facts that actually matter in 2025.

Whether you’re eyeing an electric scooter or a long-range electric car, this blog will clear your doubts with real insights, expert-backed information, and local examples.

Let’s bust the 10 most common myths about electric vehicles in India.

1. Myth: Electric Cars Have Low Range

Reality: Modern EVs offer impressive ranges suitable for both city commutes and long-distance travel. For instance:

  • Hyundai Ioniq 5: Up to 631 km range.
  • Kia EV6: Approximately 700 km range.
  • BYD Seal: Around 650 km range.
  • Mahindra BE 6: Between 557 km to 683 km range.
  • Tata Curvv EV: Offers 430 km to 502 km range. RapidEVChargE+1ZigWheels.com+1ZigWheels.com

These figures demonstrate that range anxiety is becoming a thing of the past for EV owners in India.

2. Myth: Charging Takes Too Long

Reality: Charging infrastructure and technology have significantly improved. For example:

  • Hyundai Ioniq 5: Supports fast charging, taking only 20 minutes to charge up to 80%.
  • Kia EV6: Can charge from 10% to 80% in just 18 minutes.
  • Tata Curvv EV: Charges up to 80% in 40 minutes. PBPartners

Additionally, India’s charging network is expanding rapidly, with numerous fast-charging stations available across major cities and highways.

3. Myth: EVs Are Too Expensive

Reality: While the initial purchase price of EVs can be higher, the total cost of ownership is often lower due to savings on fuel and maintenance. For example:

  • MG Comet EV: Starting at ₹6.17 lakh.
  • Tata Tiago EV: Priced at ₹7.99 lakh.
  • Tata Nexon EV: Available at ₹12.49 lakh.
  • MG Windsor EV: Starting at ₹12.00 lakh.
  • Mahindra XUV400: Priced at ₹15.49 lakh. CarWale+1CARS24+1CARS24+6The Times of India+6Navbharat Times+6

These options make EVs economically viable for a wide range of consumers.

4. Myth: EV Batteries Don’t Last Long

Reality: EV batteries are designed to last for many years. Manufacturers like Tata, Mahindra, and Hyundai offer warranties of up to 8 years on their batteries, ensuring peace of mind for consumers. Advancements in battery technology have also enhanced durability and performance over time.

5. Myth: There Aren’t Enough Charging Stations

Reality: India’s EV charging infrastructure is growing rapidly. Major cities and highways are now equipped with numerous charging stations, and companies are collaborating to expand this network further. For instance, the Ministry of Power aims to install one charging station every 25 km on both sides of highways and cities. RapidEVChargE

6. Myth: EVs Are Not Powerful

Reality: Electric vehicles deliver instant torque, providing quick acceleration and a smooth driving experience. For example:CARS24+22RapidEVChargE+22The Times of India+22

  • Kia EV6: Offers exceptional performance with quick acceleration.
  • Hyundai Ioniq 5: Known for its impressive performance and handling. PBPartners

Even budget EVs like the Tata Nexon EV and Mahindra XUV400 provide a zippy and responsive driving experience.

7. Myth: EVs Are Not Safe

Reality: Safety is a top priority for EV manufacturers. Modern EVs are equipped with advanced safety features and have achieved high safety ratings. For instance, the Mahindra BE 6 and XEV 9e come with robust safety features, ensuring passenger protection. Navbharat Times+6ZigWheels.com+6CarWale+6

8. Myth: EVs Are Not Suitable for Long Trips

Reality: With extended ranges and a growing charging infrastructure, EVs are well-suited for long-distance travel. Models like the Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6 offer ranges exceeding 600 km, making them ideal for road trips without frequent charging stops. PBPartners

9. Myth: EVs Are Not Environmentally Friendly

Reality: EVs produce zero tailpipe emissions, significantly reducing air pollution. Even when considering the entire lifecycle, including manufacturing and electricity generation, EVs have a smaller carbon footprint compared to internal combustion engine vehicles. As India’s electricity grid becomes greener, the environmental benefits of EVs will continue to grow.

10. Myth: EV Maintenance Is Complicated

Reality: EVs have fewer moving parts than traditional vehicles, leading to lower maintenance requirements. There’s no need for oil changes, and components like brakes tend to last longer due to regenerative braking systems. Routine maintenance typically involves tyre rotations, battery coolant checks, and air filter replacements. @EconomicTimes

Conclusion

Electric vehicles are becoming an increasingly practical and sustainable choice for Indian consumers. With advancements in technology, infrastructure, and a growing range of models from brands like Tata, Mahindra, Hyundai, Kia, and MG, many of the common myths surrounding EVs are being debunked. It’s time to embrace the future of mobility.
